Complete rules

1. Objective & controls

Standard matches are 11-a-side tabletop football: one keeper and ten outfield pieces. The first team to score 3 goals wins. Golden Goal Blitz ends on the first goal.

Press or touch a legal piece, drag backwards, and release to flick it in the opposite direction. A longer pull gives more power. Mobile edge flicks automatically preserve usable pull distance.

2. Formation & kick-off

Before the first kick-off, arrange each team in its own half and press Validate. Outfielders may defend inside their own penalty area, but may not enter either keeper-only strip, the centre circle, the opposing half, or overlap another piece. Keepers must remain in their goal area.

The validated formation is saved and restored after goals. Kick-offs are indirect and must be followed by a build-up touch before a goal can count.

3. Turns & competitive play

The team in possession receives up to 3 successful flicks. The flicked piece must touch the ball to continue; missing or an opponent touch ends possession.

Competitive matches enforce a 15-second action clock and a restrained trajectory guide. It shows only the initial unimpeded path and stops before any board rebound.

4. Tactical setup flicks

Before each possession change or restart, except the initial kick-off, the team about to defend receives one setup flick for a single piece. The ball is frozen while this happens.

A corner then gives the attacking team one additional setup flick. Touching an opponent during any setup flick is a foul. Touching the ball is also a foul when it is live during an open-play turnover setup.

5. Fouls, penalties & reds

If the flicked piece hits an opponent before touching the ball, the opponent receives a free kick from the foul position. A foul inside the fouler's own penalty area becomes a penalty.

If a defensive setup foul interrupts a team that already controls the ball in the attacking half, it is denial of a scoring opportunity: the fouler is sent off and the restart preserves the ball's advanced position instead of dragging the attack backward. For penalties, the box is cleared so only the taker and defending goalkeeper remain inside it.

6. Goals & legal shots

A goal counts when the required portion of the ball crosses the goal line between the posts. Own goals count for the team attacking that goal.

The ball must be in the shooting team's attacking half when struck. A direct attempt from the shooter's own half is disallowed and restarts with a goal kick. On an indirect corner or throw-in, a goal on the initial flick counts only when the first piece touched is an attacker; a defender-first deflection cannot score. Kick-offs still require a separate build-up action.

7. Restarts & out of play

Crossing a sideline gives the opponents of the last toucher a throw-in. Crossing a goal line outside the goal gives a goal kick when the attacker touched last, otherwise a corner. A throw-in leaves the receiving team one follow-up flick.

Free kicks, penalties and goal kicks are direct. Kick-offs, throw-ins and corners are indirect. On the initial goal-kick flick, the ball passes through the kicking team's own pieces but still collides with opponents, allowing a defensive line to clear the restart. Other restarts use normal collisions. After a goal or boundary crossing, the ball and player pieces finish a short natural glide before the recorded restart is resolved.

8. Keepers, goals & boards

Only keepers may occupy the keeper strip in front of goal, and no piece may enter the net. Outfielders are moved away from protected goal areas to prevent goal-line camping.

Keepers preserve their live position through misses, interceptions, saves and ordinary turnovers. A whistle or dead-ball restart resets them before either side takes a setup flick, so their position is known before the tactical decision. On a corner, the defending keeper takes the near-post position first. On penalties the defending keeper patrols the goal line. Player pieces may rebound from the outer boards; the ball is out as soon as it crosses the white pitch boundary.

9. Replays, VAR & modes

Goals receive a full build-up replay. Each side has one manual slow-motion VAR replay per match. A used VAR cannot be reclaimed.

Local 2-Player is for two people sharing one device; Showmatch vs AI is the single-player exhibition and uses the selected difficulty. Competitive standardises the restrained aim guide and action clock. Quick Five uses five pieces per team and ends at two goals. Trick Lab provides forgiving resets. Golden Blitz remains first-goal-wins. The 36 challenges use varied skill objectives.

10. World Cup 2026

All 48 participants are drawn randomly from four FIFA-ranking pots into 12 groups of four. Each team plays the other three once. A win earns 3 points, a draw 1, and a loss 0.

Tables are ordered by points, goal difference, goals scored, then FIFA rank. The top two from every group and the eight best third-place teams advance to the Round of 32, followed by the Round of 16, quarter-finals, semi-finals and final. Knockout simulations cannot finish level.

11. Tournament progression, AI levels & saves

Other matches are simulated one global matchday at a time after your group match, so no future round is revealed early. AI-vs-AI results use ranking points: stronger teams have better winning odds, while close opponents remain unpredictable.

The highest-ranked opponents use Hard AI: trained tactical weights and the best evaluated move, with strong but human mechanical execution. Normal occasionally chooses a second-best route and Easy varies both decisions and execution more noticeably. The opponent level is shown before every match.

The World Feed records completed results, while Groups and Knockouts let you browse the tournament. Cup saves are available between matches; an in-progress match must be completed before the tournament can be saved again.

12. Clubs, Seasons & European cups

Your favourite club replaces Red in ordinary modes and is stored on this device. Club home kits are used whenever possible; the away side automatically changes strip when the primary colours would clash.

Seasons is a persistent fifteen-season career across three divisions in England, Spain, Italy, Germany and France. Careers begin in the top two tiers; third-tier promotion and relegation activates after season two, and relegation into tier three ends your career. Matches last five minutes or end when either club scores twice. When 5:00 expires, the active passage continues until the next valid goal or boundary crossing. League draws then stand; a level knockout enters sudden death.

Every career opponent is Normal, Hard or Super Hard and finals are always Hard or above. Promotion play-offs, evolving club ratings, board objectives, dismissal risk, national cups and 36-club European league phases are simulated across the whole career world. Open All Leagues or Cups to browse tables, results, qualification places, brackets and archived seasons.

Champions Cup, Europa Cup and National Cup remain condensed standalone knockouts for shorter sessions.
