How to play

Goal. First to 3. 11-a-side (1 keeper + 10). Drag back from a piece and release to flick, slingshot-style.

Formation. Before kick-off, drag your players inside your own half and press Validate. The first kick-off team is shown while you set up. Outfielders cannot start inside either penalty box or the center circle; keepers must stay in the goal mouth. Your validated shape is reused after goals.

Keeper and net areas. Only keepers may occupy the small strip directly in front of either goal. No player may enter the net behind a goal; outfielders are moved back to prevent goal-line camping.

Possession. Up to 3 flicks a turn. Touch the ball with your flicked piece to keep going; miss it and you lose it. Throw-ins reset the 3-flick counter. If the ball touches an opponent (a save or block), that's a turnover too.

Fouls. Hitting an opponent before the ball is a foul -> free kick. A foul in the defending box is a penalty.

Defending. Before every possession change (except kick-off) the side about to defend gets one flick to move a single piece. On that flick, touching the ball or an opponent is a foul.

Shooting. A goal only counts if the ball was past halfway when struck. A shot from your own half is chalked off.

Boards and edge power. Player pieces may travel into the outer boards lane and bounce back, but the ball is out when it crosses the white pitch line. Flicks near the screen edge adapt their pull distance on mobile so power stays reachable without dragging off-screen.

Set pieces are taken by flicking the ball. Kick-offs, throw-ins and corners are indirect - the ball must touch another player before a goal counts. Corners give the attacker an extra set-up flick; a small defender-free pocket is cleared around every set piece so it's playable.

Out of play. Sideline -> throw-in. Goal line -> corner or goal kick depending on who touched it last.
